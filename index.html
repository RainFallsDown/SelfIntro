Academic Papers

RoboBrain 2.0: See Better. Think Harder. Do Smarter.
BAAI RoboBrain Team
arXiv:2507.02029v1 [cs.RO] 2 Jul 2025
We introduce RoboBrain 2.0, our latest generation of embodied vision-language foundation models, designed to unify perception, reasoning, and planning for complex embodied tasks in physical environments. It comes in two variants: a lightweight 7B model and a full-scale 32B model, featuring a heterogeneous architecture with a vision encoder and a language model. Despite its compact size, RoboBrain 2.0 achieves strong performance across a wide spectrum of embodied reasoning tasks. On both spatial and temporal benchmarks, the 32B variant achieves leading results, surpassing prior open-source and proprietary models. In particular, it supports key real-world embodied AI capabilities, including spatial understanding (e.g., affordance prediction, spatial referring, trajectory forecasting) and temporal decision-making (e.g., closed-loop interaction, multi-agent long-horizon planning, and scene graph updating). This report details the model architecture, data construction, multi-stage training strategies, infrastructure and practical applications. We hope RoboBrain 2.0 advances embodied AI research and serves as a practical step toward building generalist embodied agents. The code, checkpoint and benchmark are available at https://superrobobrain.github.io/.

RoboRefer: Towards Spatial Referring with Reasoning in Vision-Language Models for Robotics
Enshen Zhou, Jingkun An, Cheng Chi, Yi Han, Shanyu Rong, Chi Zhang, Pengwei Wang, Zhongyuan Wang, Tiejun Huang, Lu Sheng, Shanghang Zhang
Beihang University, Peking University, Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence
RoboRefer is the first 3D perception-enabled vision-language model specifically designed for multi-step reasoning in robotic spatial referring tasks. The model acquires precise spatial understanding capabilities through supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and achieves general multi-step spatial reasoning through reinforcement fine-tuning (RFT). The research team constructed the RefSpatial dataset, containing 20 million question-answer pairs covering 31 spatial relationships and supporting complex reasoning processes with up to 5 steps. Experimental results show that RoboRefer achieves an average success rate of 89.6% on spatial understanding tasks and is 12.4% more accurate than Gemini-2.5-Pro on the RefSpatial-Bench benchmark. The model can be integrated with various control strategies to execute long-term, dynamic tasks in complex real environments, suitable for various robotic platforms such as UR5 robotic arms and G1 humanoid robots.

CordViP: Correspondence-based Visuomotor Policy for Dexterous Manipulation in Real-World
Yankai Fu, Qiuxuan Feng, Ning Chen, Zichen Zhou, Mengzhen Liu, Mingdong Wu, Tianxing Chen, Shanyu Rong, Jiaming Liu, Hao Dong, Shanghang Zhang
Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS) 2025
State Key Laboratory of Multimedia Information Processing, School of Computer Science, Peking University, The University of Hong Kong, Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence
CordViP is a correspondence-based visuomotor policy framework for dexterous manipulation in the real world. This research addresses key issues in 3D imitation learning: the quality of point clouds captured by a single-view camera is affected by factors such as camera resolution, position, and hand occlusion, and global point clouds lack the contact information and spatial correspondence needed for fine manipulation. CordViP builds and learns correspondence by leveraging robust 6D pose estimation from object and robot proprioception, first introducing interaction-aware point clouds to establish correspondence between objects and hands. This method demonstrates excellent dexterous manipulation capabilities in six real-world tasks, achieving an average success rate of 90%, significantly surpassing other baseline methods, and showing excellent generalization and robustness across different objects, viewpoints, and scenarios.
